Accessibility

Our commitment to an inclusive digital experience

Our Commitment

121212 GBM — The Global Black Marketplace — is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all people, including individuals with disabilities. We continually work to improve the user experience for everyone and apply relevant accessibility standards to ensure we provide equal access to all users.

Standards We Follow

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible to people with a wide range of disabilities, including:

  • Visual impairments (blindness, low vision, color blindness)
  • Hearing impairments
  • Motor disabilities
  • Cognitive and neurological disabilities

Measures We Take

To ensure accessibility, 121212 GBM takes the following measures:

  • Semantic HTML markup for proper document structure and screen reader compatibility.
  • Sufficient color contrast ratios between text and background elements.
  • Alt text on images to describe visual content for assistive technologies.
  • Keyboard navigability throughout the platform — all interactive elements are accessible without a mouse.
  • Responsive design that adapts to various screen sizes, devices, and zoom levels.
  • Clear, readable typography with scalable font sizes.
  • ARIA labels and roles where needed to enhance assistive technology support.
  • Form labels and error messaging that are accessible and descriptive.

Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility, some third-party content, embedded media, or legacy elements may not yet fully meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards. We are actively working to address these areas and welcome feedback from our community.

Assistive Technology Compatibility

121212 GBM is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, TalkBack)
  • Screen magnification software
  • Speech recognition software
  • Keyboard-only navigation

Accessibility is an ongoing effort. We regularly review and update our platform to improve accessibility and comply with current standards. This statement was last reviewed on March 22, 2026.
